All you need to know about 'DEPRESSION'

Author - Abhishek
22 April. 8 min read

Sadness, feeling down, and having a loss of interest or pleasure in daily activities are familiar feelings for all of us. But if they persist and affect our lives substantially, the issue may be depression.

Depression is a mood disorder that involves a persistent feeling of sadness and loss of interest. It is different from the mood fluctuations that people regularly experience as a part of life

Depression is an ongoing problem, not a passing one. It consists of episodes during which the symptoms last for at least 2 weeks. Depression can last for several weeks, months, or years.

Do I have Depression?

We all feel down from time-to-time. Depression isn’t just about sadness, though. There are a number of other symptoms that medical professionals use to identify depression in their patients. Common symptoms of depression include:

a depressed mood

reduced interest or pleasure in activities once enjoyed

sleeping too much or too little

feelings of worthlessness or guilt

difficulty thinking, concentrating, or making decisions

The hidden signs of depression

In females:

Irritability,anxiety,mood swings,fatigue etc.

In males:

avoiding families and social situation

working without a break

having difficulty keeping up with work and family responsibilities

displaying abusive or controlling behavior in relationships

In college students:

difficulty concentrating on schoolwork, insomnia, sleeping too much, a decrease or increase in appetite.

In teens:

withdrawing from friends and family

difficulty concentrating on schoolwork

feeling guilty, helpless, or worthless

restlessness, such as an inability to sit still

Treatments for Depression

Depression is treatable, and managing symptoms usually includes::

Medication

Medication is helpful when it comes to reducing the symptoms of depression quickly, but it isn’t usually an effective treatment by itself. For that reason, other methods are usually used in conjunction with tablets

Pshchotherapy

Psychotherapy, also known as talk therapy, is sometimes used alone as the sole treatment for milder cases of depression. If cases are more severe, psychotherapy is usually used alongside medication in an attempt to deal with the root cause of the problem whilst managing immediate symptoms.

Self-Help Treatments for Depression

There are a wealth of different practices individuals can use to alleviate the symptoms of depression on their own. These include:

Take Care of Yourself

When feeling depressed, one of the first things to fall to the bottom of the priority pile is our health. The moment we start to feel down, many of us abandon our workout plan, let our sleeping pattern run wild and resort to eating junk food as a coping mechanism.

Avoid Drugs and Alcohol

Although it can be tempting to resort to drugs and alcohol when seeking to manage the symptoms of depression, this is never a good idea. Neither recreational drugs or alcohol will make your depression better they only make it worse.

Find a Focus

Whether it’s a job, hobby, project or long-term goal, having something to focus on can help to reduce symptoms of depression and provide us with a renewed sense of purpose.
